About Lincoln

Producer | Mixer | Songwriter |
Multi-Instrumentalist

Lincoln Parish is a Nashville-based producer, mixer, songwriter, and multi-instrumentalist, best known as a Grammy-nominated, founding member and lead guitarist for Cage the Elephant. Parish co-wrote and played guitar on the band’s 2011 breakout album, Thank You Happy Birthday, which debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200 and has since sold over 1.5 million copies in the U.S. alone. He also co-wrote and played guitar on the band’s 2013 Grammy nominated album, Melophobia, which debuted at #2 on the Billboard 200 and has since sold over 1 million copies in the U.S.

Although he is a Kentucky native, Lincoln's career over the last two decades has been international while living and working in England and touring around the globe. In 2013, he left the professional touring life to focus on his production company, Talk Box Rodeo and songwriting career. He has since produced and mixed music for a wide range of artists including Lilly Hiatt, Rivers and Rust, Ten Minute Detour, Rebecca Roubion, Alicia Blue, and many more. He frequently collaborates with American Idol Alum Paul McDonald, and works regularly on music for TV and film. Music featured on Gray's Anatomy, Fire Country, Dallas, Lucifer, and many more. Carrollton's "Made for This", produced by Parish, was featured in the 2018 Winter Olympics.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How do I contact Lincoln for consultation?

    Email Lincoln at talkboxrodeo@gmail.com Consultations are free to discuss your project and needs.

  • What is the production setup?

    Hybrid set up centered around an API 1608, using many of the up-to-date modern plug-ins we all love and know plus a few of the classics from the past. I print all my mixes back thru the SSL fusion to bring in additional analog warmth at the end stage of the process.

  • Can I get Lincoln to play on my album?

    Lincoln is available for session work. Pricing is based on the project and the amount of time required. Inquire for more information.

  • Why does the studio require a deposit?

    The studio requires a deposit to reserve dates for production and studio rental. Deposits are required to insure the engineers and space are covered in case of cancellation or rescheduling. The deposit is applied to the final invoice.

  • When can I expect to receive my tracks back?

    Within 7 business days, unless otherwise specified.

  • How far in advance do I need to reserve studio dates?

    The studio requires a minimum of 2 weeks notice to reserve dates. The studio is booked on a first come, first serve basis. If you are requesting specific dates, please inquire as early as possible to insure availability.

  • How would you describe your style?

    Rock and Pop influenced. Melody always wins in my book. I grew up in the early 90's and my ear has always gravitated towards big choruses,hooks and slamming drum sounds. Even as a guitar player and multi instrumentalist myself, I've always found the most important things when listening to music is the vocals and drums. If those two elements aren't right then its going to be much harder to get the rest of it to feel exciting. I want each artist's DNA to shine through on each project. It is my goal to make sure your vision is conveyed across the board and I want to be the person to help you get there.
