About Lincoln

Producer | Mixer | Songwriter |
Multi-Instrumentalist

Lincoln Parish is a Nashville-based producer, mixer, songwriter, and multi-instrumentalist, best known as the lead guitarist for Cage the Elephant. Parish co-wrote and played guitar on the band’s 2011 breakout album, Thank You Happy Birthday, which debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200 and has since sold over 1.5 million copies in the U.S. alone. He also co-wrote and played guitar on the band’s 2013 Grammy nominated album, Melophobia, which debuted at #2 on the Billboard 200 and has since sold over 1 million copies in the U.S. alone.

Lincoln has been a part of the Nashville music scene for over 15 years. His work includes producing and writing Canadian rockers Ten Minute Detour's Common Pleasure, producing and mixing Lilly Hiatt's Walking Proof, mixing Hillary Williams' Lucky Scars EP and co-producing, co-writing, and playing on country duo Rivers and Rust's album Welcome to the Show. He frequently collaborates with American Idol Alum Paul McDonald, and works regularly on music for TV and film. Carrollton's "Made for This", produced and mixed by Parish, was featured in the Winter Olympics. He has also produced and mixed music for the NFL, NBA, and NCAA.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How do I contact Lincoln for consultation?

    Email Lincoln at talkboxrodeo@gmail.com Consultations are free to discuss your project and needs.

  • Is the studio available for rent without production services?

    Yes, the studio is available for rent without production services. Inquire for pricing and availability.

  • Can I get Lincoln to play on my album?

    Lincoln is available for session work. Pricing is based on the project and the amount of time required. Inquire for more information.

  • Why does the studio require a deposit?

    The studio requires a deposit to reserve dates for production and studio rental. Deposits are required to insure the engineers and space are covered in case of cancellation or rescheduling. The deposit is applied to the final invoice.

  • When can I expect to receive my tracks back?

    Within 7 business days, unless otherwise specified.

  • How far in advance do I need to reserve studio dates?

    The studio requires a minimum of 2 weeks notice to reserve dates. The studio is booked on a first come, first serve basis. If you are requesting specific dates, please inquire as early as possible to insure availability.
